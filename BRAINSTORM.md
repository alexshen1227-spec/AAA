# The Wilds of Aerwyn - Brainstorm (July 2026)

58 ideas generated by an 8-lens design panel (quests/NPCs, combat, exploration, progression, emergent sandbox, polish, endgame structure, wildcard), each grounded in the actual `src/` modules, then scored by a 3-judge panel:

- **Fun** (weight 0.40) - moment-to-moment joy, surprise, "I have to tell someone" energy
- **Feasibility** (weight 0.35) - realistic for solo dev + Claude in THIS codebase (static hosting, no build step, 60fps budget)
- **Identity** (weight 0.25) - makes Aerwyn more distinctively itself vs. a generic open-world checklist or BotW clone

Effort: **S** = an afternoon, **M** = a day or two, **L** = a week, **XL** = multi-week.

## Delivery status

This document is the original ranked design input, not a list of missing work.
The current game has shipped or substantially adapted more than thirty of these
ideas across repeated implementation, stress-test, and polish passes.

**Shipped/adapted:** Deeds Written in Stars, Gloamings, Letters on the Wind,
The Pale Hart, Glimmerwisp Company, Kindled Arrows, Living Gusts, Zephyr Pods,
The Wind Remembers credits, The Ninth Warden, Name of the Land, Songlines,
Golem-Forged Gear, Postcards, Hollow Stones, The Same Sky, Gubbin, War Drums,
Lanterns at Mirrormere, Razorkites, Chase the Fallen Star, The Long Reed, The
Coil, The Coiled Storm, the Wayfarer's Scarf and idles, Last Light, the Hush
Bell, the Wanderer's Journal, the Simmerpot, hit-stop/hurt direction, a gem
trader, Wardshell guarding/bolt reflection, and gameplay-reactive storms.

The implementation also grew original follow-on content: the Under-Mere,
Tumbled Vale, Sixth's Vigil, The Drift, Stray Squalls, Gloamhounds, Emberside,
Scent on the Wind, thermal riders, a Warden Echo chain, and extensive locally
authored Blender upgrades for characters, wildlife, relics, and landmarks.

Ideas still below remain valid future pitches, but they should be re-evaluated
against the shipped campaign rather than assumed absent. Features that would
add backend dependence, unbounded procedural worlds, or a recognizable clone
of another game's signature mechanic were intentionally deprioritized.

## Ideas in detail (ranked)

### Deeds Written in Stars (constellation achievements)

**Score 8.3** | effort M | Structure & Endgame | fun 8 / feasibility 8 / identity 9

Achievements, but diegetic: each deed — glide the length of Mirrormere, wake all beacons, ride an updraft above the clouds, watch a blood night through to dawn — lights one star in an original constellation you can actually see in the night sky, forming the Wayfarer figure over the course of the game. Checking the sky at night to watch your own legend fill in is pure wonder-per-line-of-code.

**How:** sky.js already renders stars, meteors, and a moon halo; add ~15 fixed bright 'deed stars' (soft glow sprites) that fade in when their flag flips. Deed checks are tiny hooks in player.js (glide distance, updraft apex), world.js (beacons via G.shrines), and animals.js (deer encounters), all persisted as a deeds map in state.js. A satchel tab in ui.js lists them as verses ('The star of the long glide — found'), reusing the discovery-toast pattern.

**Judges:**
- Fun (8): Watching your own legend fill in as actual stars in the night sky is the most wonder anyone has ever wrung from an achievements system.
- Feasibility (8): sky.js already renders stars/glows and every deed maps to existing state; it just touches many files with small hooks plus one UI tab.
- Identity (9): Deeds lighting stars in an original constellation you can actually stand under is achievements transfigured into Aerwyn's own quiet sky-wonder.

### Gloamings (ghost echoes at ruins)

**Score 8.2** | effort M | Quests / NPCs / Narrative | fun 8 / feasibility 7 / identity 10

At night near the vaults, translucent amber figures flicker into being and replay ten-second moments from before the Sundering — two figures embracing at an ouroboros gate before one steps through, a golem lowering its palm so a child can climb up. No text, no voice: just light, motion, and the generative score dropping to a single tone. It is the cheapest possible 'this world had a past' punch.

**How:** Clone the hero-rig.js skeleton with an additive transparent emissive material (like the existing moonglade/light-pillar look) and hand-keyframe 3-4 short pose loops — the boglin part-driven animation code in enemies.js is the template. Trigger via proximity + sky.js night check, one-shot flags like opening.js's 'said' object. Each vignette unlocks a Chronicle entry (ties into the Hollow Stones tab). audio.js gets one 'echo' pad stinger.

**Judges:**
- Fun (8): Wordless amber ghosts replaying a lost world at night is maximum melancholy wonder for the player, pure screenshot-and-gasp material.
- Feasibility (7): Transparent-emissive rig clones are cheap, but hand-keyframing 3-4 readable ghost vignettes is fiddly animation craft on a procedural rig.
- Identity (10): Wordless amber ghosts replaying pre-Sundering moments at the vaults is the game's identity distilled — pure melancholy mystery for pennies.

### Letters on the Wind

**Score 8.2** | effort S | Quests / NPCs / Narrative | fun 7.5 / feasibility 9 / identity 8

While gliding, you spot a sheet of paper spiraling in an updraft. Snatch it mid-air and it's a letter from Piet, a skyfolk courier who fell when the islands broke — each letter funnier and sadder than the last, and the final one asks whoever finds it to deliver word to a grave cairn in Thornwood. A traversal mechanic you already mastered becomes a five-minute short story with a gut punch.

**How:** Spawn 5 fluttering paper sprites (leaf-fall particle logic in world.js already does tumbling cards) inside existing updraftZones; catching is a distance check in player.js's glide state. Reading uses ui.dialog(); the cairn is one interactable in Thornwood whose onUse plays a soft audio.js stinger and grants a unique item (Piet's Cap or a star shard). Flags persist via the seen/tut pattern in state.js. Maybe 250 lines total.

**Judges:**
- Fun (7.5): Snatching a letter mid-glide is a delightful mechanical surprise, and the grave-cairn gut punch gives a five-minute story real weight.
- Feasibility (9): Updraft zones, glide state, tumbling-card particles, and dialog are all verified; ~250 lines is a fair estimate.
- Identity (8): Funny-sad letters from a fallen skyfolk courier caught mid-glide are original, wistful, and rooted in Aerwyn's own sky catastrophe.

### The Pale Hart

**Score 8.2** | effort M | Quests / NPCs / Narrative | fun 8.5 / feasibility 8 / identity 8

At dawn, once, a white deer stands apart from the herd and does not flee — it looks at you, walks a few steps, and waits. Follow it through fog and forest light shafts to a hidden glade where a nameless overgrown shrine wakes for it, not for you. No dialog, no quest marker: just the oldest fairy-tale hook in the book, and players will screenshot it.

**How:** animals.js (12.5k) already has deer with graze/flee states — the Hart is one deer instance with flee disabled and a scripted waypoint path (the walk-toward-point steering exists for herds), spawning at dawn via sky.js dayTime once a mid-game flag is set. Destination is a hidden glade with an extra shrine reusing the world.js awaken/light-pillar sequence, plus a Hearty Mushroom ring. One-shot flag in the save; a Chronicle/Journal entry ties it into the lore web.

**Judges:**
- Fun (8.5): A white deer that looks at you and waits is the purest fairy-tale hook in games, and the shrine-wakes-for-it twist is instantly shareable.
- Feasibility (8): Deer steering and shrine-awaken FX exist; a no-flee deer on scripted waypoints gated by dayTime is a small, safe build.
- Identity (8): A wordless white hart leading to a shrine that wakes for it, not you, is fairy-tale humility that fits the serene wilderness perfectly.

### Glimmerwisp Company

**Score 8.1** | effort S | Systems & Progression | fun 7 / feasibility 10 / identity 7

The 18 hidden glimmers are pure checklist right now. At 6 found, a tiny green wisp starts trailing you; at 12, a second joins and they orbit while you idle; at 18, they glow bright enough to light your way at night like a living lantern. A collectible that visibly accretes onto your character is the cheapest 'my save is special' signal in the game.

**How:** Derive everything from G.glimmers (already saved and shown as 'x of 18' at ui.js:696) — zero schema change. Wisps are makeGlow sprites from world.js lerping toward an offset behind the player, updated in the world update loop; tier 3 parents a small PointLight active when sky.js says it's night. Maybe 80 lines total.

**Judges:**
- Fun (7): Wisps visibly accreting onto your character until they light your night walks is disproportionate charm for 80 lines.
- Feasibility (10): Derives entirely from the already-saved G.glimmers with makeGlow sprites and one night-gated PointLight; the 80-line estimate is credible.
- Identity (7): Collectibles that accrete into living lantern-wisps orbiting the wanderer is a gentle, original expression of progress that suits the tone.

### Kindled Arrows

**Score 8** | effort S | Emergent / Sandbox | fun 9 / feasibility 9 / identity 5

Draw an arrow through any lit flame — a boglin campfire, even a mage's fire bolt mid-air — and it catches, trailing embers as it flies. Burning arrows deal bonus damage, light unlit camp fire-rings from a distance (waking a camp early, on your terms), and burn away gloom flora to reveal what it hides. It's the classic 'the world is consistent' moment: the player invents fire arrows, the game never explains them.

**How:** In player.js the arrow update loop already integrates pooled arrows ({g,t,vx,vy,vz}); add a per-arrow `lit` flag set when the arrow passes within ~1m of a lit camp fire position (enemies.js camps, ~line 315, already track fire ring positions and lit state for the night lighting). Visuals: swap the arrowhead to an emissive material + one shared pooled PointLight + reuse the campSmoke ember-puff pool. On hit, pass `lit` through the existing enemy hit path in enemies.js for a damage bonus, flip unlit camp fires to lit, and call a `burnAt(x,z)` hook on gloom flora in world.js.

**Judges:**
- Fun (9): Discovering fire arrows yourself because the world is simply consistent is the exact unscripted 'I invented this' magic that defines the genre.
- Feasibility (9): The arrow pool struct and camp fire positions/lit state are verified; a per-arrow lit flag, emissive head, and burnAt hook are all small.
- Identity (5): The unexplained world-consistency discovery is immersive and burning gloom flora ties in, but fire arrows themselves are stock Zelda vocabulary.

### Living Gusts

**Score 7.9** | effort S | Emergent / Sandbox | fun 7.5 / feasibility 9 / identity 7

The curling TotK-style wind streaks that already sweep the meadow stop being decoration: catch one while gliding and it shoves you along its path in a satisfying surge, with the grass wave, leaf fall, and butterflies all bending to the same wind. Suddenly the player reads the sky like a sailor — waiting on a gust line to stretch a glide to a far island.

**How:** world.js already maintains a `gusts` array (~line 2759) of traveling wind-line effects; give each gust a world-space direction, speed, and capsule bounds. In player.js's glide branch (next to the shared updraft contract at line 789), add horizontal acceleration when inside a gust capsule; feed the same direction/phase into the grass shader's gust term (world.js:1403) so physics and visuals agree. Nudge butterflies and leaf-fall particles in animals.js/world.js with the same vector for a few extra lines.

**Judges:**
- Fun (7.5): Making decorative gusts physically real teaches players to read the sky like sailors, an elegant quiet delight.
- Feasibility (9): The gusts array exists at world.js:2759 and the glide/updraft contract is in player.js — adding a direction capsule and one shader term is genuinely S.
- Identity (7): Making the existing wind streaks rideable teaches players to read the sky like a sailor — wind-as-character is deeply Aerwyn.

### Zephyr Pod — a bottled updraft you can plant anywhere

**Score 7.9** | effort M | Emergent / Sandbox | fun 8 / feasibility 9 / identity 6

A rare seed pod harvested beneath the sky islands: hurl it and it bursts into a swirling column of petals and wind that lasts ~12 seconds — a personal, portable bellows. Players use it to cheese cliffs, escape a boglin ambush straight up, chain mid-air throws for improvised sky ladders, or (combined with Emberwake) fan a fire's direction. It turns vertical traversal into a resource the player spends creatively.

**How:** Add it to the discovery satchel (ui.js icons + state.js save flags, same path as Swift Feather); 'use' throws it with the exact ballistic math of player.js fire() (reuse tmp-vector aim code, ~line 555). On landing, push a G.updraftZones entry with `expires: G.time + 12` — the bellows already proves temporary zones work end-to-end (world.js:2574), and the base dust-swirl system renders harder swirls for expiring zones automatically (world.js:2627). Spawn pods as pickables under sky islands and in Thornwood via the existing interactables registry.

**Judges:**
- Fun (8): A throwable updraft invites endless creative misuse — sky ladders, ambush escapes, fire-fanning — which is emergent gold.
- Feasibility (9): Expiring updraftZones are proven end-to-end including the dust-swirl VFX and pruning; throw ballistics and satchel plumbing already exist.
- Identity (6): A petal-bursting bottled updraft extends the bellows/sky-island fiction into a creative tool without feeling borrowed.

### The Moonglade Stair: Mirrormere's drowned vault

**Score 7.8** | effort M | Exploration & World | fun 8.5 / feasibility 6 / identity 9

By day you can glimpse broken pillars under Mirrormere's surface. On clear nights, when the moonglade already shimmers on the lake, a spectral staircase of moonlight rises from the water and lets you walk down into an air-pocket ruin beneath the lake — a third pressure-plate vault, silver-lit, with a relic chest and a glimmer napping inside.

**How:** world.js's buildVault() pressure-plate system and makeChest() do the interior almost for free; place the vault chamber below WATER_Y in the lake basin the terrain.js heightfield already carves at (-170,120). The stair is an emissive toonMat mesh toggled by G.dayTime plus the clear-weather check on G.weather, using the same night gating the moonglade/leaping fish already use. A fade transition (reuse opening.js letterbox fade) hides the walk through the water plane.

**Judges:**
- Fun (8.5): A moonlight staircase rising from the lake into a drowned ruin is a secret so good players will drag friends to the shore at night.
- Feasibility (6): buildVault/makeChest do the interior cheaply, but an air pocket below WATER_Y fights the swim/drown logic and water plane assumptions with hidden edge cases.
- Identity (9): A moonlight staircase descending into a drowned silver vault deepens Mirrormere's nocturnal magic and the vault mystery in one unmistakably Aerwyn image.

### The Wind Remembers Your Name (credits)

**Score 7.8** | effort S | Structure & Endgame | fun 6.5 / feasibility 9 / identity 8

After the serpent falls, dawn breaks for the first time in a hundred years and the camera drifts over everywhere you wandered — Mirrormere, the Thornwood, the woken beacons — while credits roll. The final title card answers the elder's opening line: 'and the wind remembered our names.' A small build that makes the whole game feel authored.

**How:** Reuse opening.js's letterboxed, skippable cinematic rig with a hardcoded list of camera waypoints (all landmark coordinates already exist as constants in world.js). Credits text is an HTML overlay in ui.js like the existing banner/title screens; audio.js's generative music already supports slow dawn crossfades. Set a G.finished flag persisted in state.js save() so the title screen can show a completion mark.

**Judges:**
- Fun (6.5): Dawn breaking over everywhere you wandered is a real lump-in-throat payoff, but it is one three-minute moment.
- Feasibility (9): Letterboxed skippable cinematic rig, landmark constants, HTML overlays, and the save flag all exist — this is assembly, not construction.
- Identity (8): First dawn in a hundred years drifting over everywhere you wandered, answering the elder's opening line, is authored melancholy closure that could only be Aerwyn's.

### Waystones of the Departed

**Score 7.8** | effort M | Wildcard | fun 9 / feasibility 7 / identity 7

Press a key anywhere and the game carves a waystone: a glowing cairn encoding your position, a chosen glyph-mood, and your last ten seconds of movement into a ~300-character string you can paste to a friend (or share as a URL fragment). Paste someone else's string and their spectral echo appears in YOUR world — a translucent wayfarer replaying their glide off that cliff, their sprint across the moonglade. Asynchronous ghosts, zero backend: multiplayer feeling smuggled through the clipboard.

**How:** Add a 5Hz position/anim-state ring buffer in player.js (~50 samples, quantized to 2 bytes per axis over WORLD_R=480 from terrain.js — ~250 bytes, base64'd). Register pasted stones as world.js interactables with a makeGlow cairn; the ghost is a second hero-rig.js build with a transparent cyan toonMat, scrubbing the decoded buffer on loop. main.js parses location.hash on boot and adds a paste handler; ui.js shows the 'A wayfarer passed this way' toast. state.js persists received stones.

**Judges:**
- Fun (9): Pasting a string and watching a friend's translucent ghost replay their glide off your cliff is clipboard-smuggled multiplayer that players will not shut up about.
- Feasibility (7): Genuinely zero-backend via clipboard/URL and the ghost rides hero-rig, but encode/decode plus anim-state replay scrubbing is honest new M work.
- Identity (7): Spectral echoes of departed wayfarers gliding through your lonely valley suit the melancholy beautifully, even if ghost-traces echo Souls and Journey.

### The Ninth Warden (central mystery arc, three acts)

**Score 7.7** | effort XL | Quests / NPCs / Narrative | fun 8.5 / feasibility 5 / identity 10

The game's disconnected wonders — kneeling golems, beacon shrines, ouroboros gates, floating islands — snap into one question: eight Wardens once held the sky aloft; where is the ninth? Act 1: lore stones and Gloamings seed the question. Act 2: gathering three warden sigils wakes a golem, which rises, walks to a vault door, and opens it (spine-chilling set piece). Act 3: ride the great updraft to a sky island for a letterboxed reveal — the empty ninth pedestal is yours. The whole existing world becomes foreshadowing retroactively.

**How:** Act structure lives in a quest.js module gating on flags already saved (shrines/towers arrays, lore flags). The golem set piece upgrades the dormant golems in world.js with a scripted waypoint walk — reuse enemies.js's walk-home locomotion, no combat AI needed (this makes the backlog 'golem AI encounter' concrete as a scripted narrative beat first). The finale reuses opening.js's letterbox cinematic system and an updraftZones entry (already in state.js) strong enough to launch the player to the existing sky-island GLB. Sigils are three relic-chest-style interactables placed at framed vistas.

**Judges:**
- Fun (8.5): A dormant golem rising and walking to open a vault door is a spine-tingling set piece, and making the whole world retroactive foreshadowing is top-tier narrative wonder.
- Feasibility (5): Every ingredient exists, but three acts of scripting, a golem walk set piece, and a cinematic finale is real content volume for a hobby XL.
- Identity (10): The Ninth Warden fuses golems, beacons, gates, and sky islands into one question — it retroactively makes the whole world coherent Aerwyn lore.

### Name of the Land (place-name reveals with vista pan)

**Score 7.7** | effort M | Feel / Audio-Visual Polish | fun 7 / feasibility 9 / identity 7

Cresting the ridge into Thornwood or reaching Mirrormere's shore for the first time triggers a slow letterboxed camera pull, the region's name fading in as hand-lettered calligraphy while a short harp-gliss stinger plays in the current musical key. It turns the map's regions into moments players screenshot and remember.

**How:** world.js already defines named regions (Thornwood, Mirrormere, sky islands); add cheap radius/bounds checks with one-shot flags persisted in state.js's save schema (same pattern as the dialog one-shot flags). The camera pull reuses the G.cinematic flag that opening.js already uses to own the camera ('opening beats own the camera', player.js line 786), plus opening.js's letterbox DOM. Campfire dousing lives in enemies.js's updateCamps, keyed off wetness, dimming the fire light and shrinking boglin aggro radius.

**Judges:**
- Fun (6.5): Rain-doused campfires enabling gloom raids is great emergence, but climb-slip pulses risk reading as friction, not drama.
- Feasibility (7): Purely consumers of the verified single-writer G.weather — four small features that are individually easy but collectively an L of tuning.
- Identity (2): Slippery climbs in the rain is BotW's single most infamous mechanic, and douse-the-campfire stealth follows it straight down the clone path.

### The Ashen Vow (NG+ master mode)

**Score 5.6** | effort M | Structure & Endgame | fun 4.5 / feasibility 8 / identity 4

Post-credits, the ouroboros gate offers a whispered bargain: begin again under the Ashen Vow. You keep your satchel, hearts, and stamina, but boglins strike harder and roam farther, blood nights come often, and a single new item — the Ash Feather — marks your save. Cheap to build, but it doubles the game's lifespan for players who loved the traversal.

**How:** A G.vow flag in state.js's save schema; enemies.js applies damage and aggro-radius multipliers and biases spawns toward its existing tough/moss variants off that one flag. sky.js raises blood-night frequency when the vow is set. The Ash Feather rides the existing discovery-satchel pipeline in ui.js and assets.js ('New item!' toast, 3D-rendered icon) so the mode announces itself diegetically.

**Judges:**
- Fun (4.5): Harder-numbers NG+ is retention math, not wonder, and it overlaps the Ouroboros Vow idea almost entirely.
- Feasibility (8): One vow flag driving existing damage/aggro/variant/blood-night parameters, plus one item through the proven satchel pipeline.
- Identity (4): A stat-multiplier hard mode is generic; the diegetic Ash Feather is a nice touch but it lacks the thematic weight the other Vow pitch has.

### Mirror Vales: Worlds Behind the Gates

**Score 5.4** | effort XL | Wildcard | fun 7 / feasibility 3 / identity 6

The two ouroboros gates finally open — into the Mirror Vales, dreamlike pocket worlds generated entirely from a seed. Each vale is a strange sparse sibling of Aerwyn: unfamiliar mountains, a displaced lake, off-palette grass, a few seeded chests and boglin camps, no handcrafted landmarks. The seed lives in the URL (?vale=7F3K), so players trade coordinates like folklore: 'go to vale ANKA, there's a natural arch over the water at dusk.' Infinite shareable worlds from a static site — the analytic terrain's superpower, cashed in.

**How:** Parameterize terrain.js: a valeHeightAt(seed) that offsets the fbm/ridge inputs from noise.js and swaps the hardcoded landmark terms (spawn plateau, lake position) for seed-derived ones; buildTerrain/buildWater/renderMapImage already consume the height function generically. Reuse world.js's heightAt/slopeAt-gated scatter paths (grass instancing, groves, chests) while skipping bespoke landmarks; enemies.js camp spawner runs seeded. Gates become interactables that stash G world registries, rebuild, and restore on return; main.js reads ?vale= for direct links. Grass/terrain rebuild already runs at boot, so regeneration cost is a loading moment, not a frame hitch.

**Judges:**
- Fun (7): Trading vale seeds like folklore is a magical social hook, but landmark-free procedural siblings risk feeling empty next to the handcrafted valley.
- Feasibility (3): world.js is full of hardcoded landmark coordinates and has no teardown/rebuild lifecycle for its registries — seeded pocket worlds are a refactor of the whole boot path, not a feature.
- Identity (6): Dream-sibling worlds behind the ouroboros gates honor the gates' mystery, but seeded landmark-less terrain dilutes the handcrafted painterly identity toward procedural filler.

### The Wayfarer's Folio (photo compendium)

**Score 5.3** | effort L | Systems & Progression | fun 6 / feasibility 6 / identity 3

A Farsight Lens found in a relic chest turns the existing over-shoulder aim into a viewfinder: frame a buck, a boglin warrior, a waking beacon, a sky island — click — and its page fills in a leather-bound folio with your actual screenshot. Suddenly deer herds, rabbits, and shooting stars are quarry, and completionists get a reason to be out at blood-night. Milestone pages pay out gems and one final secret.

**How:** Reuse the RMB over-shoulder camera path in player.js with a vignette overlay in ui.js; on capture, frustum + distance check against registries that already exist (G.enemies kinds, animals.js herds/rabbits/birds, G.shrines, G.towers, golems, islands) to tag entries. Thumbnails come from renderer.domElement.toDataURL downscaled to ~96px JPEG stored under a separate localStorage key to protect the main save. Folio panel is a new tab beside the ui.js stats page; capture flags persist via a folio object added to state.js.

**Judges:**
- Fun (6): Photographing quarry re-motivates the wildlife, but the folio itself is completionist menu work.
- Feasibility (6): No showstopper, but toDataURL capture, localStorage thumbnail management, and frustum-tagging across every registry add up to a genuine L.
- Identity (3): A photo compendium of fauna and landmarks is the Hyrule Compendium nearly one-to-one.

### Wayfarer's Tally (speedrun timer + end stats)

**Score 5.2** | effort S | Structure & Endgame | fun 3 / feasibility 10 / identity 2

A quiet play-clock and deed tally: the pause panel shows elapsed time, and the credits end on a stats card — beacons woken, chests opened, glimmers found, deaths, time. Speedrunners get a legible target and completionists get a reason for one more lap; the game-over screen already counts beacons, so the pattern exists.

**How:** Add playTime and deaths to G and to the save()/load() schema in state.js, accumulating dt in main.js's loop. ui.js's showGameOver() already renders a beacon count into 'gameover-beacons', so the stats card extends the same pattern; chest and glimmer counts already live in G.gems/G.glimmers and the vault/chest handlers in world.js. An optional window.__stats hook mirrors the existing window.__step test convention.

**Judges:**
- Fun (3): A play clock and stats card serves a tiny niche and produces no in-world joy whatsoever.
- Feasibility (10): Two counters in the save plus extending the existing gameover-beacons stats pattern — the cheapest idea on the list.
- Identity (2): A speedrun clock and stats card is meta scaffolding with zero bearing on the world's tone or mystery.

## In-flight: The Coiled Storm (finale drop, judge-vetted July 9 2026)

Spec agreed with design-judge pass (verdicts A/B SHIP, C/D REWORK applied, E SHIP with cut):

- **Storm at (0,360)**, manifests only when `coilCompleted && !finaleCompleted`; rebuilt from flags on load. Layered translucent cones/discs (depthWrite off, distance-faded to 2 layers), dark ground shaft, local lightning. Visible map-wide.
- **Approach (r<90)**: forced storm via a new sky.js `requestWeather(kind)` lock (no stacked multipliers — single lock, released on exit); radial-out headwind scaling toward the wall, zero in the eye (r 12). One **lull shrine** checkpoint (~0,315): still-air bubble, sets respawn. Two rousable vents give forward hops. Cap the slog: full approach ~90s.
- **Eye**: dead-still air, fall speed capped at -8 ("the storm holds its breath" — no fall deaths, judge's rage-prevention rework). Spiral of 4 updraft columns (ground→35, 30→60, 55→85, 80→110) at 90° intervals, r 8. Optional 2 razorkites if the spawn API is trivial.
- **Heart (y~105)**: standing disc; orbiting sky-debris ring around a dark core; socket takes the Warden's Sigil (consumed). Socketing does NOT still the storm — it **ignites a final ouroboros ring**; the player must glide one full circuit (angular accumulation ≥ 2π in the r 8–22 band, storm's own tangential wind assists) to close the loop. The ouroboros eats its tail; then the stilling.
- **Stilling (~20s)**: rotation lerps to 0, layers peel upward and fade, weather lock → clear, chord resolves major; map-wide sky_debris meshes (names Debris_*) glow amber ~8s (emissive tint on existing instances, no new FX). `finale_completed`; storm swaps for a small serene sky-isle (grass disc + young tree). All idempotent from flags.
- **Credits "The Wind Remembers"** (NO "your name" text — the star is the name): letterboxed drift over spawn meadow → first beacon → Mirrormere → the Coil; ≤5 stat lines (days, beacons, glimmers, letters, deeds); ends on the night sky, one new brightest star kindles, then the three words. Space skips. Replayable by interacting at the serene isle. Triggered ~6s after the stilling.
